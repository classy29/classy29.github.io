vinyl cutting

Exploring Roland GX24

A vinyl cutter is a type of computer-controlled machine. Small vinyl cutters look like a desktop printer. Like a printer controls a nozzle, the computer controls the movement of a sharp blade over the surface of the material. This blade is used to cut out shapes and letters from sheets of thin self-adhesive plastic (vinyl). The vinyl can then be stuck to a variety of surfaces depending on the adhesive and type of material.

To cut out a design a vector-based image must be created in a software program (usually Adobe Illustrator or Corel Draw).

It is then sent to the cutter where it cuts along the vector paths laid out in the design. The cutter is capable of moving the blade on an X and Y axis over the material, cutting it into any shape we are able to design but there will be always one perfect settings for each design depends on the geometry. Since the vinyl material comes in long rolls, projects with significant length like banners or billboards can be easily cut as well.

Laser cutting


in Laser cutting we are assigned to make one One press fitting model using the concept of Parametric modeling.

Parametric modeling

Parametric design is a process based on algorithmic thinking that enables the expression of parameters and rules that, together, define, encode and clarify the relationship between design intent and design response.

Parametric design is a paradigm in design where the relationship between elements is used to manipulate and inform the design of complex geometries and structures.

The term parametric originates from mathematics (parametric equation) and refers to the use of certain parameters or variables that can be edited to manipulate or alter the end result of an equation or system.

in conclusion Parametric design helps designer to modify the design very efficiently , in simple words i take one dimention and take it as a main parameter which effects other parameters directly or indirectly , i will try to relate it with other dimentions as a set of equation , which i already have to follow in simple modeling also , this set of equation will help to redesign the model quickly , if i change one parameter, all dimentions related to it will change , in complex design there can be more than one parameter which are main or call it dominant in design.

in Maths dominant parameters can be called Independent parameter and other parameters as Dependent parameters, because their values depend on other parameters.


How it works ?

i prepared some basic model of parametric modeling to understand how i can use it in proper way , i initially used Constrains to have relationd between vertices , these constrains dont follow any expression , they follow simple geometric rules. List is below

  • parallel rule - lines will be parrallel to each in design
  • Horizontal/verticle rule - lines will be horizontal or verticle no in between angle will be there.
  • perpendicular rule - two line will be perpendicular to each other.
  • Symetric rule -design will be symetric to respective axis
  • equal rule - two line will be equal to each other.

    Press fitting

    Press fitting is joining of the two parts without any fastners and glue , but only friction will be there. friction will help the modules to be connected. you can know moree about pressfitting in Fab academany-tutorials. they explained whole process of pressfitting.

    KERFING

    For press fit cutting i have to make one comb for measuring kerfing. kerfing is removal of material by the the laser in process, so it will be always dimension difference in the parts you want to join from the original one. for componset the removal of the Material , one may have to add or substract dimention from original one on cad.

    to measure kerf of the material
  • cut one squre on laser , measure its dimention by calliper
  • substract original(CAD) dimention with measured one
  • Devide the value by 2, it will kerf of the material.

    • Comb test

    To know which dimention is good for the material Comb test is necessary. In comb test original dimention may Increment in some fraction (i.e., make some at w-.002″, w-.001″, w+.001″, w+.002″ etc. w is original dimention) Use kerf value on increment/decrement. and after cutting you can test each slot by fitting in each one. Try to figure out which dimention is good to apply on the design and select it.

    In my case i was working on the material which thickness is 3.25 mm - MDF
